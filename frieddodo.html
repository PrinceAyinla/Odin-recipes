fried Plantains (Dodo)

Every Cookout Need Some Delicious Dodo!

Fried ripe plantains have a crispy, caramelized texture and irresistibly sweet taste.
you are missing out if this goodness have never been experience.

What to Know before you begin :

  • Plantains are a member of the banana family. Unlike a banana, plantains are starchy and need to be cooked before eating.
  • Frying a fully ripe plantain quickly in oil coaxes all the sugar to the surface where it's caramelized.
  • The keys to successful fried plantains are choosing ripe fruit and using the right pan, oil, and heat.

Before we start Grab your ingredients

  • 5 medium sized Roma tomatoes
  • 2 ripe plantains
  • 3 tablespoons oil (such as canola or vegetable oil), for frying
  • Sea salt, optional
  • Any other added seasoning of your choice

    • Let's get cooking step by step



    1. Peel plantains by cutting off both ends, and cutting a slit down the side of each plantain.
    2. Remove the plantain peel by peeling it side to side rather than lengthwise. It will come off in sections. You may need to use the knife at the edge of each section to help loosen it from the flesh.
    3. Cut the peeled plantains into 1/4-inch-thick slices. Diagonally is preferred because it provides a larger surface for caramelization; it can be cut straight across into rounds.
    4. Drizzle just enough oil into a nonstick skillet to coat the bottom of the pan, and place it on medium heat.
    5. When the oil begins to shimmer, but not smoke, add plantains (work in batches). Fry for 1 1/2 minutes on one side, flip and cook for 1 minute on the other side.
    6. Remove plantains from the pan and drain on paper towels.
    7. Continue frying in batches until all the plantains are fried.
    8. Sprinkle lightly with sea salt to give a sweet and salty taste to your fried ripe plantains.
    9. Serve and enjoy.
